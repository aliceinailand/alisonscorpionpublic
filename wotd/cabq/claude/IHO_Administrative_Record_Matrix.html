§ 2 — IHO Rules Rules Governing the City of Albuquerque Independent Hearing Office (IHO)

Effective: March 20, 2025 (date of last signature). Recommended: Ethan Watson, City Clerk. Approved as to form: Lauren Keefe, City Attorney. Approved: Dr. Samantha Sengel, CAO. DocuSign Envelope ID: 697E3C80-E133-401F-B9B9-C85185DBFEF1. Authored by: Christopher Peck, Public Hearing Officer. Note: These rules were authored for the IHO process — Andrew Coon (Managing City Attorney) and Amanda Morefield (Code Enforcement Supervisor) appeared at the Feb 20, 2025 public hearing to shape them.

§ 1
TITLE: These Rules may be referred to as the Independent Hearing Office Rules, IHO Rules, Office of Administrative Hearing Rules, or OAH Rules.
§ 2.1
AUTHORITY AND SCOPE: Promulgated pursuant to ROA 1994, §2-7-8-3 ("IHO Ordinance").
§ 2.2
The IHO Rules provide general practice rules for all proceedings before the IHO.
§ 2.3
In the event the IHO Rules conflict with any provision of the IHO Ordinance, the IHO Ordinance shall control.
§ 2.4
CRITICAL RULE: The evidentiary and procedural Rules apply in the absence of a specific provision in any ordinance governing IHO proceedings. If a more specific law applies to the hearing type, that more specific regulation shall control. In the event that any IHO Rules conflict with any specific provision of law establishing a hearing and appeals process, such specific provision shall control.
§ 3.1
DEFINITIONS — "Appellant" or "Petitioner": The person or entity filing an appeal or initiating an action with the IHO.
§ 3.2
"Appellee" or "Respondent": The person or entity responding to or answering to the appeal or action.
§ 4.1
FILING AN APPEAL: Appellant shall file a written request for hearing and notice of appeal using the IHO form labeled "Request for Hearing and Notice of Appeal" or a substantially similar form in accordance with §2-7-8-7 ROA 1994. Appeals filed through an electronic citation platform shall be accepted.
§ 4.2
Upon filing, IHO personnel shall endorse the document with time, day, month, and year. IHO staff may only reject an appeal filed with the incorrect office (e.g., one that should be in District Court).
§ 4.3
Appeal marked as filed on the business day IHO receives it. Electronic appeals submitted after 5:00 PM shall be considered filed the next business day.
§ 4.4
Hearing officer may provide written notice when appellant files incomplete/deficient appeal. If party fails to cure deficiency within 15 days, IHO may dismiss without prejudice.
§ 5.1
FILING OF PLEADINGS: All pleadings, motions and other papers shall be filed: (a) by mail to Office of City Clerk, P.O. Box 1293, Albuquerque, NM 87103; (b) in person at 600 2nd St. NW, Albuquerque, NM 87102; (c) by email at summonsandappeals@cabq.gov; (d) online at cabq.gov/clerk/administrative-hearings.
§ 5.2
All pleadings, motions and other papers shall be in writing and shall state with particularity the grounds and the relief sought.
§ 5.3
SERVICE REQUIREMENT: The moving party shall provide a copy of all filed pleadings, motions and other papers to all opposing parties.
§ 5.4
Before submission of any motion or continuance request, the requesting party shall make reasonable efforts to consult with the opposing party about their position. The party shall state the opposing party's position in the motion.
§ 5.5
Unless a different deadline applies or is ordered by the hearing officer, the opposing party has 15 days to file a written response to a pleading. Weekend/holiday deadlines roll to next business day.
§ 5.6
Pleadings, motions and other papers marked as filed on the business day IHO receives the document. Electronic submissions after 5:00 PM not marked as filed until next business day.
§ 6.1
FAILURE TO RESPOND/ANSWER: Failure to file a response to any request for relief or motion may be presumed to be consent to the relief sought. Hearing officer not required to make such default ruling if relief would be contrary to the hearing officer's view of facts or law.
§ 7.1
REPRESENTATION AT HEARING: Authorized representatives: (a) a party to the action; (b) an authorized agent if party is entity/business; (c) attorney authorized to practice in New Mexico (must file formal written entry of appearance with IHO); (d) union representative on behalf of member(s).
§ 8.1
PREHEARING CONFERENCES: Hearing officer may conduct upon request of either party or at their discretion, requiring parties/attorneys/representatives to provide status information on a proceeding.
§ 8.2
CRITICAL: Prehearing conferences conducted by the hearing officer shall be recorded.
§ 8.3
CRITICAL: The hearing officer may enter in the record a written order reciting results of a prehearing conference, including rulings and directions to parties. The hearing officer's order shall control the subsequent course of the proceeding, unless modified.
§ 9.1
HEARING LOCATION: All hearings may occur by telephone, videoconference, or equivalent electronic method. In-person hearings at 600 2nd St. NW or another location selected by hearing officer. Hearing officer may recess electronic hearing and reconvene as in-person when necessary for full/fair process.
§ 9.2
NOTICE OF HEARING: IHO shall notify parties by mail of date, time, and place at least 7 days before the scheduled hearing, unless applicable law requires a shorter period. Notice directed to address on the notice of appeal and request for hearing.
§ 10.1
BURDEN OF PROOF: Unless otherwise specified by law, burden of proof in an administrative proceeding before the IHO is the preponderance of evidence.
§ 10.2
The party with the burden of proof ordinarily presents their case first, followed by the opposing party.
§ 10.3
Hearing officer may require or allow opening statements. Opening statements are not ordinarily evidence, but may be adopted as evidence by sworn oath of the party-witness who made the opening statement.
§ 10.4
CRITICAL — OATH REQUIREMENT: All testimony must be given under oath and will be subject to questioning of the opposing party. The hearing officer may also ask questions of the witness as appropriate.
§ 10.5
Parties may make closing arguments, either orally at the conclusion of the case or in writing after conclusion of the hearing upon order of the hearing officer.
§ 10.6
Hearing officer may require further briefing on any issue and proposed findings of fact and conclusions of law. No decision-writing deadline commences until parties submit any ordered post-hearing briefing.
§ 11.1
EVIDENCE: NM Rules of Evidence and Civil Procedure shall not apply before the IHO unless otherwise expressly and specifically required by law, regulation, or order of the hearing officer per ROA 1994, § 2-7-8-9(A). Irrelevant, immaterial, unreliable, or unduly repetitious evidence may be excluded. Hearing officer shall consider and give appropriate weight to all relevant and material evidence admitted in rendering a final decision.
§ 11.2
EXHIBIT SUBMISSION: All exhibits shall be submitted to the IHO at least 3 days prior to any hearing; producing party shall provide copies to all opposing parties. Methods: email summonsandappeals@cabq.gov; mail to P.O. Box 1293, ABQ 87103; in person at 600 2nd St. NW.
§ 11.3
Exhibits properly labeled. Appellee/Respondent labels using numbers (Exhibit 1, 2, 3). Appellant/Petitioner labels using letters (Exhibit A, B, C). Exhibits submitted less than 3 days before hearing may be admitted at hearing officer's discretion.
§ 11.4
Any party wishing to submit a video or audio recording must provide a complete tangible, playable copy that can be retained by the IHO as part of the administrative record.
§ 11.6
Hearing officer may take administrative notice of facts not subject to reasonable dispute. A party is entitled to respond as to the propriety of taking administrative notice, including the opportunity to refute a noticed fact.
§ 11.7
Parties objecting to evidence shall timely and briefly state the grounds for the objection.
§ 12.1
WITNESSES: Any person having relevant, material knowledge may testify under oath.
§ 12.3
EXPERT WITNESSES: Party intending to call an expert witness shall file a witness list at least 5 days before scheduled hearing and provide to opposing party and IHO, including: identity, scope of testimony, credentials, and materials reviewed. Opposing party may challenge designation before or during hearing.
§ 13.2
EX PARTE PROHIBITION: The hearing officer is prohibited from engaging in any ex parte communications about the substantive issues with any party on any matter. Exception: not ex parte for hearing officer to go on the record with only one party when the other party has failed to appear at a scheduled hearing.
§ 14.1
EFFECTIVE DATE: These rules became effective March 20, 2025 (date of last signature — Dr. Sengel), and shall be filed in the Office of the City Clerk.

IHO Rule § 9.2 — Impossible Deadline — CRITICAL DISCREPANCY

The October 31, 2025 Order (¶9) states that Gates was instructed to provide information to the IHO "on or before October 30, 2025." The Order itself was not issued until October 31, 2025. The administrative record therefore reflects a deadline of October 30 imposed in an Order dated October 31 — the deadline preceded the written order by one day. This is inconsistent with IHO Rule § 9.2 and due process requirements. The October 31 Order then cites (¶10) that "the requisite information has not been received" — measured against a deadline that had already passed before the order was drafted.

IHO Rule § 8.2–8.3 — Reversal Without Recorded Order

The August 26, 2025 Order explicitly found: (1) fire allegations dismissed; (2) substandard determination NOT upheld. The October 31, 2025 Order shifts course toward upholding the substandard determination based on "lack of electric service" without a recorded prehearing conference or written order explaining the reversal of the August 26 findings. Rule § 8.2 requires prehearing conferences to be recorded. Rule § 8.3 requires written orders reciting results. No such recording or written order appears in the administrative record prior to the October 31 Show Cause Order.

IHO Rule § 5.3 — Service / Response Discrepancy

Gates' on-record testimony states he emailed code enforcement and both departments with no response received. The administrative record as produced does not reflect written acknowledgment or response to Gates' 20+ written submissions. Rule § 5.3 requires the moving party to serve opposing parties with all filed pleadings, motions and papers. The IHO Rules also contemplate service flowing in both directions under § 5.5 (15-day response period).
